THE PRINCIPLE

On-device by default.
Nothing phones home.

Disk scans, telemetry, the safety checks before a delete, even the AI prose — all of it runs on your Mac. No analytics, no account, no server. conso makes no network calls of its own; the only network it ever touches is the one you point an app updater at.

01 · MENU BAR

It lives in your menu bar.

A glance gives you live CPU, GPU, memory, and network sparklines and a thermal read-out. Below them, one-tap Quick Actions: Keep Awake, Clean Keys, Clean Screen, show Hidden Files — and a jump straight to Quick Clean or Optimize.

04 · DOCTOR

It explains your Mac, in plain words.

Press the stethoscope and the Doctor reads your Mac back to you — a health score, a plain headline, and a few specific findings. Built on Apple's Foundation Models, but the rule is facts → words: conso gathers the facts, the model only phrases them. It never decides and never runs anything, and falls back to hand-written copy with Apple Intelligence off.

05 · ASK CONSO

Ask in plain English. ⌘K

Hit ⌘K and ask about your Mac — "why is it slow?" conso answers from real telemetry, then offers up to two one-tap actions you confirm. It only ever runs its own maintenance tools, never arbitrary commands, and the same "What's this?" explainer sits on every Clean / Software / Optimize item.

NON-SANDBOXED · NO SUBSCRIPTIONS

Software
A real app inventory that routes each update to the right place — Homebrew, the Mac App Store, Sparkle, or softwareupdate. Filter by App, Library, or System.
Optimize
A curated “fix a problem” catalog — rebuild Spotlight, flush DNS, clear font caches. Everything is situational and default-off; nothing runs unless you ask.
Privileged helper
The few root tasks go through a tiny SMAppService daemon with a strict, hardcoded whitelist — no shell, no arbitrary arguments, and a code-sign-validated XPC link in both directions.
Scheduled auto-clean
Optionally let Quick Clean run on a schedule — off by default, with the same preview and safety guard. It catches up on overdue runs at launch and never touches anything outside the safe set.
◆ Private by design

Your Mac's data never leaves it.

conso is a single native binary — no Electron, no web view, no phone-home. It collects no analytics and makes no network calls of its own. The privileged helper only ever runs commands from its hardcoded list, and the Apple-Intelligence advisor turns facts into words without anything leaving your machine. It's clean-room and MIT-licensed — read every line.
